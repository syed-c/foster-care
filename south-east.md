Types of Fostering in South East England
The South East sees a diverse range of fostering needs, and every carer plays a crucial role.
Short-Term and Long-Term Fostering
Short-term fostering offers care during transitions. Long-term fostering provides a stable home until adulthood or independence.
Emergency and Respite Fostering
Emergency foster carers help children who need immediate support.
 Respite carers offer short breaks to families or other carers.
Therapeutic and Specialist Fostering
Some children need more emotional support. Agencies provide specialist training and guidance so you feel confident and prepared.
Parent and Child Fostering
You support a young parent and their baby, helping them develop parenting skills in a safe environment.

How to Become a Foster Carer in South East England
If you're wondering how to become a foster carer in South East England, the process is structured, supportive, and designed to help you succeed.
Initial Contact and Home Visit
Your chosen agency speaks with you and arranges a home visit to learn about your life, routine, and caring environment.
Training and Assessment Process
You attend preparation training and meet with an assessor who helps you explore your strengths and readiness.
Fostering Panel and Approval
A panel reviews your assessment. Once approved, you can be matched with children who need your care.
You’re always supported—every step is explained gently and clearly.

South East England Foster Care FAQ
Do I need experience to foster?
 No. Agencies provide training and ongoing guidance.
Can I foster if I rent?
 Yes—stable housing and space for a child are what matter.
Are carers paid in the South East?
 Yes. Allowances vary by agency and the child’s needs.
How long does approval take?
 Around four to six months for most people.
Can I foster if I work full-time?
 Yes, depending on placement type and your support system.
